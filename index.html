Mentorship

Black in Al has a graduate school mentorship program pairing those applying to graduate schools with mentors who provide feedback, present information sessions, and guide mentees throughout their application process.

Financial Support

Black in Al awards need-based travel grants to attend our annual workshop and the NeurlPS conference. Our grants cover flights, accommodation, daily per diems, visa fees, and registration for the NurlPS conference. In 2018, 188 works from 28 countries were presented at BAI with a total of $375k given out in travel grants supporting 284 people.

Advocacy

Our advocacy on barriers faced by our members ranging from visa issues while attending major Al conferences, to the lack of a presence by international companies on the African continent has resulted in new processes by conferences such as NeurlPS to mitigate barriers faced by our members, a major international Al conference (ICLR) to be held in Ethiopia (the first time such a conference is held in Africa), and the first Google Al center in Africa (Accra, Ghana).
